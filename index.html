My Favorite OSINT Tool and Why

One OSINT tool I have found especially interesting is Shodan. Unlike a normal search engine that indexes websites, Shodan indexes internet-connected devices and services such as servers, routers, webcams, and remote desktop systems.

What makes Shodan valuable from a cybersecurity perspective is how quickly it demonstrates the importance of proper security configuration. It can identify exposed services, open ports, software banners, SSL certificate information, and other details that organizations may unintentionally leave visible online.

I also like that Shodan connects cybersecurity concepts to real-world infrastructure. Instead of only reading about vulnerabilities and exposure management, it becomes possible to see examples of internet-facing systems and understand why security hardening matters.
